- **RESULTS: PASS (minor presentation discrepancy — gap column).** Prod lists 6 rounds (Australia 20 Feb, Portugal 27 Mar, Netherlands 17 Apr, Hungary 1 May, Czech 15 May, Aragon 29 May), each with Race 1 + Superpole Race + Race 2 cards = exactly the 6 rounds run (next: Misano 13–14 Jun, per Wikipedia calendar — prod's "next session ~1 day away" agrees). - **Source solidity:** `lib/standings/wsbk.ts` + `lib/results/wsbk.ts` → api.wsbk.pulselive.com (official Dorna vendor JSON:API, sibling of the MotoGP feed; Chromium UA). Fragility: single vendor API behind CloudFront (NASCAR-class datacenter-IP/WAF block is the live risk); plus the now-demonstrated `time`-field semantic assumption (cumulative vs gap) — the one place the loader interprets rather than relays.

Sources: official site https://www.24h-rennen.de/en/2026/05/17/final-report/ + RACER + dailysportscar. For a single-event series, the past-winners list IS the flagship data surface, and it is 25 days stale on the page's own headline event — prod's own calendar card even shows the (passed) May date alongside a winners list that pretends 2025 was the latest edition. Fix: prepend one row to `content/series/adac-ravenol-24h/champions.json` ("year": 2026, drivers Engel/Martin/Schiller/Stolz, "Winward Racing Team RAVENOL — Mercedes-AMG GT3"). Rows that ARE shown verify clean: 2025 Rowe BMW, 2024 Scherer PHX Audi, 2023 Frikadelli Ferrari 296 — all match the official record.
- **Source solidity:** fully curated (`champions.json`, `sessions.json`, `history.md`) — zero parser/scrape risk, which is exactly why the 2026 row didn't appear by itself: curated single-event data has no upstream to refresh it and no in-code freshness check. Same structural lesson as the WEC-2024 curated error: curation failures are silent. A single-event series needs a "curate the new winner" step in the post-race-weekend routine.

Immediate attention, in priority order:

1. **`content/series/adac-ravenol-24h/champions.json`** — prepend the 2026 row (Engel/Martin/Schiller/Stolz — Winward Racing Team RAVENOL, Mercedes-AMG GT3). One line; the flagship surface of a single-event series is 25 days stale on its own headline event.
2. **WRC champions tab** — curate `content/series/wrc/champions.json` with separate `constructor` (driver's make) + `constructorChampion` fields. The generic parser maps Wikipedia's manufacturers'-champion column into the driver's inline team slot — actively WRONG in every split-title year (2024, 2020, 2019…), not just missing.
3. **NASCAR champions tab** — curate `content/series/nascar-cup/champions.json` (2025 Larson/Chevrolet, 2024 Logano/Ford, 2023 Blaney/Ford, …). Dead surface today, same class as the Formula E finding.
4. **Cross-series pattern:** `lib/wikipedia-champions.ts` is now 2 dead (formula-e, nascar-cup) + 1 wrong-column (wrc) out of the 4 audited series that rely on it (nls is the only clean one). Recommend a standing rule: every series gets a curated `champions.json`; the generic parser should be a last-resort fallback only.
5. **WSBK gap column** (`lib/results/wsbk.ts` ~line 150) — Pulselive's `time` field is cumulative for ALL riders; compute P2+ gaps as `time − winner.time` instead of prefixing "+". Minor but every results card currently shows "+54-minute" gaps.
6. NLS results/standings: no action required for correctness, but the Verstappen-season newsworthiness makes the "Coming soon" tabs a visible gap; cheapest first step is `officialStandingsUrl` in `content/series/nls/meta.json` → free link-out card.
